Why is my washer
not working?

Your washer won’t start, what to do now? That can be a big hassle, especially when you have a pile of laundry waiting to get washed. Take a look at some possibilities for the cause of this issue:

01. Power Cord

First ensure that power is getting to the electrical outlet. If the electrical outlet is receiving power, but the washer won’t start, the power cord might be defective. However, this is very rare.

02. Display Board

The display board might need to be replaced, or there could be a problem with the power to the board.

03. Line Fuse

The line fuse blows if the washer circuits are overloaded. If the line fuse blows, the washer won’t start.

04. Incoming Power Problem

The electrical outlet may not be getting power. To determine if the outlet is getting power, try plugging something else into the outlet. If the outlet is not getting power, check the home circuit breakers or fuses.

05. Door Lock

The door lock secures the washer door shut during operation. Once the washer door is closed, the washer can begin operating. If the washer door is not latched shut, the door switch will prevent the washer from operating. If the door lock is defective, the washer will not spin or start.

06. Main Control Board

The main control board might be defective. However, the main control board is rarely at fault for this symptom.

Why is my washing machine not draining?

There could be a clog in the drain, or a damaged drain pump or hose that need replacement. Moreover, If the lid switch assembly fails, it prevents the washer from spinning when the lid is open, and the washer will not drain. Lastly, some older washing machines are equipped with a water pump belt. If the water pump belt is broken or worn out, the washer will not drain.

Why is my washer not spinning?

If your washer doesn’t spin, there might be a part that needs replacement. Some parts that may be faulty are:

01. Lid Switch Assembly

The lid switch assembly prevents the washer from spinning when the lid is open. If the lid switch assembly fails, the washer will not spin.

02. Drive Belt

If the drive belt is broken or loose, it needs replacement.

03. Motor Coupling

If the washer is overloaded, or because of normal wear, the motor coupling could fail.

04. Clutch

The clutch assembly makes the connection between the transmission and the inner tub. It helps the tub to gradually get up to the proper spin speed. If the clutch is worn out, it may prevent the tub from spinning.

05. Drive Pulley

The drive pulley connects to the washer drum and works with the drive belt and motor to rotate the drum. If the drive pulley is worn out, the drive belt might slip off, preventing the washer from spinning.

Why is my washer leaking?

Leaks around the washer are usually caused by a leaking hose. You have three hoses on a washer — two supply hoses and one drain hose. They might be disconnected or damaged and need replacement. Moreover, water could leak from your machine Because of a worn out tub seal or door boot seal.

Why is my washer isn’t filling with
water or fills too slowly?

If your machine isn’t filling with water, there’s probably a problem with the water control or inlet valve, or one of the hoses. Moreover, the home water pressure might be too low. The water inlet valve requires at least 20 psi to function properly. Check the water pressure to the valve to determine if it is sufficient.
